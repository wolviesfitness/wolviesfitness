3 Years of Coaching: The 5 Things That Actually Determine Results
AB
Albin Binu
Feb 12, 20265 min read

After 3 years of coaching calisthenics, nutrition, and rehabilitation to over 500 clients across India, patterns have emerged. The clients who transform and the clients who quit are not separated by genetics, age, starting fitness level, or even how much they train. They're separated by 5 specific behaviours.

1. They Show Up When They Don't Want To

Motivation is unreliable. It comes and goes based on sleep, stress, mood, and a hundred other things outside your control. The clients who transform have built a system — a specific time, a specific place, a non-negotiable routine — that runs independent of how they feel that day.

2. They Track Something

Doesn't have to be calories or reps. Could be a simple daily checkbox. But the act of tracking creates accountability and makes progress visible. Progress that you can see — even if it's just "I trained 4 days this week vs 2 last week" — creates the motivation to continue.

3. They Ask for Help Quickly

The clients who plateau the least are the ones who message me when something feels off. Not 3 months later after they've entrenched a bad habit. The ego cost of saying "I don't understand this" is much smaller than the time cost of training wrong for months.

4. They Prioritise Sleep

Nobody wants to hear this but it is the single most underrated factor in physical transformation. Muscle is built during sleep. Fat is burned during sleep. Hormones that regulate hunger and recovery are regulated during sleep. 7-9 hours is not optional — it's training.

5. They Think in Months, Not Weeks

Every client who has quit has done so because they expected faster results than biology allows. Every client who has transformed has had a 3-6 month mindset from day one. If you go in expecting to see dramatic change in 4 weeks, you will be disappointed. If you commit to 6 months, you will be amazed.
